The Lagoon Penthouse

Victoria Island, Lagos
  • Up to 6 guests
  • 3 bedrooms, 3 beds

Set on the upper floors of a quiet residential tower off Ozumba Mbadiwe, this three-bedroom apartment looks directly over the lagoon towards Ikoyi. Floor-to-ceiling glass runs the length of the living room, and the water changes character through the day — working boats at dawn, the bridge lights after dark. The building has a generator on permanent standby, borehole water, and a manned gate, so the practical business of Lagos stays quietly handled while you are here.

Inside, the apartment is calm and unfussy: a long sofa facing the view, a dining table that seats six, and a kitchen equipped for actual cooking rather than gesture. Two of the three bedrooms face the water. It suits a family returning for a few weeks, or two couples travelling together who would rather share a home than a hotel corridor.

Home truths

  • i

    The third bedroom is genuinely smaller than the other two and takes a double rather than a king. It works well for a child or a single traveller.

  • i

  • i

About Victoria Island

Victoria Island is where Lagos does its business and, increasingly, where it eats. Once a residential district of low-rise houses and wide streets, it now carries most of the city's corporate towers, the better restaurants, and a growing number of galleries. The result is a neighbourhood that runs on a weekday rhythm — busy and formal until seven, then loosening considerably.

For a returnee it has one obvious practical virtue: almost everything you will want is within a short drive, and the roads that matter are among the best maintained in the city. Ozumba Mbadiwe and Ahmadu Bello run the length of the island and are lit through the night. Restaurants cluster around Akin Adesola and Adeola Odeku, both a few minutes from the apartment.

It is not a quiet neighbourhood in the way Ikoyi is quiet. There is construction, there is traffic, and there is noise on Friday nights. What it offers instead is proximity — to the airport road, to the island's restaurants, and to the lagoon itself.

Getting here from the airport

Roughly 55–75 minutes outside peak. Allow two hours if you land between 4pm and 8pm on a weekday.

  1. Murtala Muhammed International to Ikeja

    Leave the international terminal and join the airport road towards Oshodi. Dual carriageway the whole way, resurfaced and well lit.

    • 15 min
    • Good road
    • Lit at night

  2. Ikeja to Third Mainland Bridge

    Through Oshodi and onto the bridge approach. This is the stretch that determines your journey time — free-flowing at midday, slow from mid-afternoon. Surface is sound but uneven in places.

    • 20–40 min
    • Moderate road
    • Lit at night

  3. Third Mainland Bridge to Ikoyi

    The long crossing over the lagoon. Well maintained and fast once you are on it. Lighting is good across the span, with a short unlit section near the Adekunle exit.

    • 15 min
    • Good road
    • Mostly lit

  4. Ikoyi to Ozumba Mbadiwe

    Across Falomo Bridge onto Victoria Island, then right along the lagoon front. Smooth surface, heavy signage, busy but orderly.

    • 10 min
    • Good road
    • Lit at night

  5. Ozumba Mbadiwe to the apartment

    Turn off onto a short residential side street. The last hundred metres are paved but narrow, with speed bumps. The gate is manned around the clock and the security team holds your name on arrival.

    • 3 min
    • Paved, narrow
    • Gate lit
